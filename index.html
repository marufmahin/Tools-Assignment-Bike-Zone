Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most important things I should know about riding a bike?
Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic signals, signs, and laws. Get in the mindset of “driving” your bike—not just “riding” your bike. This will help you be a more focused and legally compliant bike rider.
How can I tell if my helmet is old and I need a new one?
If the foam feels soft, the straps are loose, or there are visible cracks, it's time to replace your helmet. In general, it's best to get a new one every 5 years.
My bike has been in storage is it safe to ride?
Before riding, check the brakes, tires, chain, and lights. Clean the bike thoroughly, and if needed, get it serviced before hitting the road.
What rules should I follow when riding my bike?
While riding a bike, wearing a helmet is essential. Using bike lanes is recommended. When turning, it's important to signal with your hand. Avoiding mobile phone use is wise. At night, keeping your lights and reflectors on ensures safety.
